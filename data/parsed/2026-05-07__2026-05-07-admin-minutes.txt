2. Department Business 
a. Community Justice 
1. FOPPO Collective Bargaining 
Scott Hyde, Community Justice Director, requested authorization from the Board to begin preliminary collective 
bargaining discussions with the FAPO bargaining unit, identified as the Parole and Probation Officers 
Association. Hyde explained that the bargaining unit is unique to Community Corrections and currently consists 
of approximately fifteen to sixteen members. He further advised that the existing labor agreement is set to expire 
June 30, 2026. 
Hyde explained that the initial bargaining sessions would primarily involve technical and procedural contract 
discussions rather than substantive economic proposals. He stated that historically, the County had begun 
preliminary negotiations internally before involving outside legal counsel, unless negotiations advanced toward 
arbitration or more complex matters. 
Discussion occurred regarding the County’s transition to new employment legal counsel and the importance of 
including Human Resources, Finance, and legal counsel in communications throughout the negotiation process. 
Commissioner Martin advised that while initial conversations and information gathering could proceed, 
substantive proposals or agreements should not be finalized until legal counsel had been fully incorporated into 
the process.  
Hyde stated that the intent was to begin preliminary discussions, identify bargaining topics, and return to the 
Board with proposed redlined changes and recommendations at the appropriate stage of negotiations. 
Commissioners expressed support for beginning the preliminary bargaining discussions and encouraged 
Community Justice staff to continue acting as good stewards for the County during negotiations. 
No formal motion was made. The Board provided consensus authorization for Community Justice to begin 
preliminary bargaining discussions consistent with Board direction. 
b. Forestry 
1. Out of State Travel Request 
Forestry Director Tom Streeter presented a request for approval of out-of-state travel for Brian Braddock, Forester II, to 
attend a weed control and herbicide application seminar in Burney, California. Streeter explained that attendance would 
assist Braddock in maintaining required pesticide and chemical applicator licensing credits while also providing 
opportunities to review herbicide application trials and network with other forestry professionals. 
 
Discussion occurred regarding whether similar certification opportunities were available online. Streeter advised that while 
online opportunities do exist and have been utilized previously, the in-person seminar would provide more practical field-
related training and networking opportunities. Commissioners also briefly discussed forestry-related facilities and herbicide 
vendors associated with the seminar. 
 
The Board approved the out-of-state travel request without objection.

c. Conklin Avenue Townhomes Plat Map 
The Board reviewed the proposed Conklin Avenue Townhomes plat map. Discussion occurred regarding recent state middle 
housing regulations intended to encourage increased housing density through smaller lot sizes. Commissioners noted that 
the proposal involved four smaller residential lots within one of the County’s older neighborhoods and discussed the 
importance of expanding housing opportunities within the community. 
Commissioners additionally discussed: 
• 
Support previously expressed by the Urban Planning Commission regarding middle housing concepts  
• 
Site layout and access considerations  
• 
Compatibility with the surrounding neighborhood  
• 
The need for additional housing development opportunities within the County 
Commissioner Martin made a motion to approve the Conklin Avenue Townhomes plat map. Commissioner Richardson 
seconded the motion. Upon roll call; Commissioner Richardson – yes, Commissioner Martin – yes, Motion carried 2-0. 
4. OTHER 
Commissioner Martin introduced discussion regarding a proposed personal services contract with former Josephine County 
Finance Director Arthur O’Hare to serve in an interim finance support capacity during the transition following Ruth 
Nelson’s retirement. Ruth Nelson and Arthur O’Hare joined the Board for discussion regarding the proposed contract. 
Commissioners discussed review of the contract by Legal Counsel, including provisions related to contract structure, scope 
of services, scheduling expectations, and independent contractor status. Arthur O’Hare explained that the compensation 
structure was based on the Finance Director salary schedule at a mid-range step equivalent and estimated approximately 
twenty hours per week of services. O’Hare outlined his anticipated schedule and responsibilities, which included: 
 
Monday availability to organize and prepare weekly finance and budget matters 
Attendance at Tuesday and Thursday budget meetings and Administrative Workshops 
Availability to County personnel for finance-related guidance and continuity support 
Assistance with the County budget process through June 30, 2026 
 
Discussion also occurred regarding the importance of maintaining continuity during the budget process, flexibility in 
scheduling, and O’Hare’s prior experience serving sixteen years with the County, including ten years as Controller and six 
years as Finance Director. Commissioners expressed appreciation for O’Hare’s willingness to assist the County during the 
transition period.  
Commissioner Martin made a motion to approve the personal services contract with Arthur O’Hare as presented. 
Commissioner Richardson seconded the motion. Upon roll call; Commissioner Richardson – yes, Commissioner Martin – 
yes, Motion carried 2-0.
