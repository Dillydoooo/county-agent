2. Department Business 
a. Public Health 
1. Approve the Grant Request so it can be submitted for consideration to the Solid Waste Agency 
Michelle Shaw presented a proposed grant request prepared by Public Health Director Janet Jones for submission to the 
Solid Waste Agency. Shaw explained that the funding would assist the County in reestablishing and supporting solid waste 
abatement activities and program operations. 
 
Discussion was held regarding the proposed funding amount of $155,000 and whether the request represented an annual 
allocation or a total amount distributed over a three-year period. Commissioners expressed concern that the language within 
the application materials lacked clarity regarding the duration and structure of the funding. Finance Director Ruth Nelson 
noted that in prior years the County had received approximately $155,000 annually through similar funding arrangements. 
 
Commissioners discussed the importance of obtaining clarification from the Solid Waste Agency while still moving the 
request forward to avoid delays in restoring the County’s solid waste abatement program.  Commissioner Martin made a 
motion to approve submission of the grant request with clarification requested regarding whether the $155,000 amount 
represented annual funding or total funding over a three-year period. Commissioner Richardson seconded the motion. 
Upon roll call; Commissioner Richardson – yes, Commissioner Martin – yes, Commissioner Smith – yes. Motion carried 
3-0. 
 
Prior to moving to the next agenda item, discussion occurred regarding a pending personnel action associated with the solid 
waste program. Michelle Shaw explained that approval would be needed to move forward with a personnel action involving 
a returning solid waste specialist lead position. Commissioners discussed the timing relative to the supplemental budget 
process and expressed support for proceeding with the personnel action once procedural requirements were satisfied. 
 
b. Human Resources 
1. Discuss Current Employee Retiring from PERS. Approval needed from the Board. Josephine County 
Personnel Policy Article 4, C.2.B 
Tamra Martin, Recreation Director, presented a request for approval to allow a retiring PERS employee to return temporarily 
to County employment on a part-time basis. Martin explained that the employee currently serves as the department’s only 
CDL-qualified operator and possesses significant institutional knowledge necessary to complete the White Horse renovation 
project. 
 
Martin stated that the proposal would allow the employee to return on a temporary, part-time schedule of approximately 24 
hours per week, primarily to complete remaining project work through the grant deadline at the end of August. She explained 
that the position was budgeted and intended solely as a temporary transition measure. 
 
Discussion occurred regarding the state’s allowance for retired PERS employees to return to work under limited 
circumstances, including both the benefits and potential concerns associated with such arrangements. Commissioners noted 
that the temporary nature of the assignment and the specialized expertise involved made the proposal appropriate under the 
circumstances presented.  
Commissioner Martin made a motion to approve bringing back the temporary project position associated with the retiring 
PERS employee. Commissioner Richardson seconded the motion. Upon roll call; Commissioner Richardson – yes, 
Commissioner Martin – yes, Commissioner Smith – yes. Motion carried 3-0.

            a. Finance – Finance Director, Refill Vacating Position, Vacating May 2026 – ORS 
192.660(2)(a) 
Discussion was held regarding the upcoming vacancy in the Finance Director position due to the announced retirement of

8. Open Session Cont’d 
Upon returning to open session, Chair Smith summarized that the Board had received updates, held discussions, and 
provided direction to staff on the Executive Session personnel matters. The Board additionally determined that one agenda 
item would be tabled until the following week. 
Commissioner Martin introduced discussion regarding former Josephine County Finance Director Arthur O’Hare 
potentially serving in an interim capacity following the upcoming retirement of Finance Director Ruth Nelson on May 19, 
2026. Commissioner Martin stated that she had previously worked with O’Hare and believed his prior experience with the 
County would be beneficial during the transition period and ongoing budget process. Discussion occurred regarding the 
possibility of structuring the arrangement as an independent contractor agreement, subject to review and preparation by 
Legal Counsel. Arthur O’Hare addressed the Board and stated that he previously served sixteen years with the County, 
including ten years as Controller and six years as Finance Director. O’Hare explained that while he was not interested in 
returning full-time, he would be willing to assist on a part-time basis to support the budget process, provide guidance to 
finance staff, and assist with continuity during the transition.  
Commissioners discussed the importance of maintaining continuity through the budget adoption timeline ending June 30, 
2026, and expressed appreciation for both O’Hare’s willingness to assist and Ruth Nelson’s service to the County. 
Commissioner Martin additionally stated that she would like O’Hare to participate in the hiring panel process for the 
permanent Finance Director position. 
No formal action was taken during the discussion. Staff was directed to continue working with Legal Counsel regarding 
contract development and next steps 
 
The meeting adjourned at 2:20 p.m.
