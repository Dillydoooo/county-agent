Budget Committee: Ron Smith(absent), Colene Martin, and Gary Richardson, Marc Kirby, Chris Collins and

1) Open Meeting – Colene Martin, Vice-Chair, Board of County Commissioners 
Commissioner Martin opened the meeting at 9:00 a.m.  
2) Declaration of Potential Conflicts of Interest 
All members declared that there is not a conflict of interest on today’s agenda. 
3) Establish new Chair and Vice-Chair for the Budget Committee 
Ms. Martin opened the floor for nominations to fill the Chair and Vice-Chair positions for the new board.  
Colene Martin made a motion to elect Gary Richardson as Chair of the Budget Committee, seconded by 
Dorothy Yetter. Upon Roll Call- Ms. Yetter – Yes, Mr. Collins – Yes, Mr. Kirby –Yes, Mr. Richardson – 
Yes, Ms. Martin – Yes. Motion carried 5-0. 
Gary Richardson made a motion to elect Marc Kirby as Vice-Chair of the Budget Committee, seconded by 
Colene Martin. Upon Roll Call- Ms. Yetter – Yes, Mr. Collins – Yes, Mr. Kirby –Yes, Mr. Richardson – 
Yes, Ms. Martin – Yes. Motion carried 5-0. 
4) Budget Officer presents the Fiscal Year 2025-26 Supplemental Budget 
Ruth Nelson, Budget Officer and Finance Director, presented the Fiscal Year 2025–2026 Supplemental 
Budget materials to the Budget Committee. Nelson explained that supplemental budgets are used to amend 
appropriations after adoption of the annual budget when unexpected circumstances arise or when new 
appropriations or transfers become necessary. She further explained that while Oregon budget law does not 
require Budget Committee approval for supplemental budgets, Josephine County Charter requires Budget 
Committee review and approval. 
Nelson reviewed Exhibit A and Exhibit B contained within the budget packet and explained that the 
supplemental budget before the Committee primarily addressed reinstatement of the County’s Solid Waste 
Program under the Public Health Department. 
Nelson stated that the proposed supplemental budget would: 
• 
Transfer $31,000 from the General Fund  
• 
Reinstate one FTE position for the remainder of Fiscal Year 2025–2026  
• 
Cover approximately two months of operating expenses through June 30, 2026  
She further explained that the estimated annual cost of the program is approximately $176,000, including 
personnel and materials and services. Nelson clarified that the County is currently applying for a Solid Waste 
Agency grant expected to provide approximately $155,000 annually beginning July 1, 2026, if approved.  
Nelson additionally clarified discussion from the prior Board meeting, confirming that the proposed grant 
amount would be $155,000 per year rather than a total amount spread across multiple years. Chair Richardson 
and Commissioner Martin provided additional background regarding the County’s decision to return solid waste enforcement 
responsibilities to the Public Health Department. Discussion focused on distinctions between public health enforcement matters 
and traditional code enforcement or building code violations handled through Community Development.  
Commissioners explained that the reinstated position would primarily address environmental and public 
health-related complaints, including: 
• 
Sewage discharge issues  
• 
Trash accumulation creating health hazards  
• 
Disease vector concerns such as rodent infestations  
• 
Conditions impacting neighboring properties or waterways  
Budget Committee Meeting – Friday May 30, 2025 – 10:00 a.m. 
Commissioners emphasized that the position would not focus on building code enforcement, land use 
violations, or general construction compliance matters.  
Discussion also occurred regarding public perception of prior enforcement structures and concerns 
previously expressed by members of the community regarding consolidation of enforcement authority within 
Community Development. Commissioners noted the importance of clearly separating environmental public 
health enforcement from land use and building code enforcement responsibilities.  
5) Public Comment (limit of three minutes each) 
No public testimony was provided. 
6) Deliberate on Supplemental Budget 
Budget Committee members discussed the supplemental budget and asked questions regarding the history 
and future operation of the Solid Waste Program. 
Chris Collins asked why the position had previously been removed and whether reinstating the position 
would result in future staffing concerns or backlash. Commissioners discussed historical concerns related to 
property rights perceptions and prior organizational changes that consolidated certain enforcement activities 
into Community Development.  
Committee members additionally discussed: 
• 
Existing backlog of complaints  
• 
Whether one employee would be sufficient to address enforcement demands  
• 
Potential future administrative support needs  
• 
Differences between environmental health enforcement and building code enforcement  
• 
The need for improved complaint routing systems within County operations and the County website  
Commissioners noted that the prior Solid Waste Program had historically involved more than one staff 
member and acknowledged that future staffing needs may need to be evaluated during the upcoming annual 
budget process.  
Discussion also occurred regarding the County’s supplemental budget process generally, with Nelson 
explaining that this represented the third supplemental budget hearing of the fiscal year, with one additional 
supplemental budget expected before the close of Fiscal Year 2025–2026.  
7) Motion to Approve Supplemental Budget 
Chris Collins made a motion to approve the Fiscal Year 2025–2026 Supplemental Budget as presented, 
seconded by Dorothy Yetter. Upon Roll Call- Ms. Yetter – Yes, Mr. Collins – Yes, Mr. Kirby –Abstained, 
Mr. Richardson – Yes, Ms. Martin – Yes. Motion carried 4-1. 
 
8) Adjourn 
The meeting adjourned at 1:53 p.m.

RE: 
Supplemental Budget for FY 2025-26 – Solid Waste 
When the budget is adopted by the Board of County Commissioners it sets the legal expenditure 
limitations by appropriation. These limitations are detailed in the appropriation resolution. When 
unexpected circumstances arise, appropriations may need to be increased or decreased, transferred 
from one appropriation category to another, or new appropriation categories created. Supplemental 
budgets are the method used to amend the budget after the budget is adopted. 
Per Oregon budget law the budget committee is not required to approve supplemental budgets, but it is 
required per the County charter. The Budget Committee is scheduled to review and approve the 
supplemental budget Wednesday May 6, 2026, at 1:30 PM in the Anne Basker Auditorium as noticed in 
the Daily Courier and on the Josephine County website. The opportunity for public testimony on the 
proposed supplemental budget will be provided at this meeting. Citizens are also encouraged to submit 
comments in writing, either by mail to the Board of County Commissioners Office or by email to 
bcc@josephinecounty.gov.   
Two exhibits follow this memo. Exhibit A (page 2) shows the new appropriations as required by Oregon 
Budget Law for any appropriations with a change. You will also see a brief description of the reason for 
the change. Exhibit B (page 3) shows the originally adopted appropriations for the entire County budget, 
the changes to the appropriation, and the new appropriation. Below is a summary of the changes:  
•
The Board of County Commissioners agreed to bring back the Solid Waste Department under
Public Health. This Supplemental Budget is to reinstate Solid Waste operations before the end of
FY 2026. This is funded by a $31,000 transfer from the General Fund and covers the cost of the
program for just the remaining two months of the fiscal year.
•
The annual cost of the program is estimated to be $176K for FY 2027 including personnel costs
as well as materials and services. The County is in the process of applying for a grant from the
Solid Waste Agency in the amount of $155K for future years minimizing the use of General
Funds.
The total increase to the FY 2026 County budget for this supplemental only is $31,000 and 1 FTE. Once 
the supplemental budget is approved by the Budget Committee it goes to the Board of County 
Commissioners for adoption. The supplemental budget hearing is tentatively scheduled for May 13, 
2026 at 9:00 AM with the opportunity for public testimony.
