WINTER 2026
A missive from the Applegate
Partnership & Watershed Council
APWC has hit the ground running in 2026! Read on
for project progress across each of our programs.
Reconnecting a Historic Side Channel on the
Applegate River
This winter, APWC is starting work on the Applegate
River Mile 25 Side Channel Restoration Project near
Applegate, Oregon. The project will restore ~0.65
miles of side channel on private properties along the
river.
Side channels are smaller channels that branch off
from a river and are important habitat for juvenile
salmon. They provide slower water, refuge during
high flows, and areas where young fish can feed and
grow. Since construction of Applegate Dam, much of
this off-channel rearing habitat has been eliminated
in our watershed. 
Like many of our restoration projects, this work will
take place within a river floodway and requires
surveying and hydraulic modeling and goes through
multiple levels of permitting. Professional designs
help us understand how the river will respond during
high flows and changing conditions. Design work
will continue through 2026, with a goal of
construction in 2027.
Good news from Cheney Creek
For the past three winters, APWC has partnered with
a fisheries biologist to conduct spawning surveys on
Cheney Creek. These surveys track adult salmon and
count redds, the nests female salmon create in
stream gravel to lay their eggs.
This winter brought especially encouraging results.
Redd counts jumped from 16 last year to 116 this
winter, more than six times higher than the previous
season. Adult observations also increased
dramatically, rising from 11 to 44, roughly four times
as many adults as last year. Despite relatively low
stream flows this season, Cheney Creek saw a strong
surge in spawning activity.
Spawning surveys are one way we measure how fish
populations are responding to habitat conditions
over time. While ocean cycles and other factors
influence annual returns, this strong increase is a
promising sign for the watershed.
The next challenge for these young fish will be
surviving the low flows and warm temperatures of a
Southern Oregon summer so they can grow strong
enough to begin their journey to the ocean.
Community Wildfire Protection Plan
The second round of Community Wildfire Protection
Plan (CWPP) public meetings took place last month
in Ruch, Applegate, Williams, Murphy, and
Wilderville. APWC and partners presented maps of
potential fuels treatment areas based on responses
from the CWPP public survey and analysis from the
Vibrant Planet decision-making tool. The third and
final round of public meetings will be held in April
and early May. The goal of those meetings will be to
present the draft CWPP document to residents,
before it is sent off for editing in early summer. 
 
Ta k e  t h e  C W P P  S u r v e y 
Fuels reduction work expands
Fuels treatment implementation funded by the
recently secured US Forest Service IRA grant is
progressing quickly, with impressive results. BurnBot
was selected as the contractor and they have met or
exceeded all expectations. APWC also obtained two
Oregon Department of Forestry Small Forestland
Grants to expand the UAAWRP footprint, support
local Firewise Communities, and to begin a pilot
study on the best practices and costs associated with
Douglas Fir mortality mitigation.
Cantrall Buckley Park cleanup
APWC will be conducting cleanup work at Cantrall
Buckley Park throughout the winter, following the
felling of hazard trees by Jackson County Parks. This
work is helping to make the park a safer and more
enjoyable place of recreation for our community.
The Education Program continues to grow
APWC’s Education Program is getting ready for a
packed spring and summer! 
First up is Applegate Outdoor School (AOS). We will
be hosting 580 6th graders from the Medford
School District over eight weeks in April and May.
Each 6th grader will experience a week of outdoor,
inquiry-based learning at Cantrall Buckley Park. 
Next, we are excited to announce that Applegate
Outdoor Summer Camp (AOSC) will be returning this
summer! Registration for our six-week season opens
March 16th.
