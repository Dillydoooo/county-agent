2. Solid Waste Discussion 
Frank Wharregard and James Black from Community Development, Janet Fredrickson from Public Health, Ruth 
Nelson, Finance Director, Trent Carpenter, Solid Waste, and Michelle Shaw, Human Resources Director, Wally 
Hicks and Leah Harper, Legal Counsel. 
 
The Board convened a comprehensive discussion regarding the current status of solid waste enforcement within 
Josephine County and the need to re-establish a functional program. The discussion included participation from 
representatives of Community Development, Public Health, Finance, Legal Counsel, Human Resources, and Solid 
Waste. 
 
Chair Smith opened the discussion by identifying the growing number of public complaints related to solid waste 
issues and the absence of a formal enforcement structure. It was noted that the County currently does not have a 
code enforcement officer or an established system to address solid waste violations, despite continued receipt of 
complaints from the public. 
 
Legal Counsel, through Leah Harper, provided a detailed historical overview of the County’s solid waste and code 
enforcement program. She explained that, historically, enforcement responsibilities were handled by a single 
individual within Public Health who primarily worked toward voluntary compliance and, in some cases, pursued 
enforcement through hearings officers. Over time, enforcement efforts evolved into a system that included cleanup 
by the County followed by cost recovery through liens or legal action against property owners. This created a 
revolving funding mechanism that allowed the County to address additional cases. However, that system 
ultimately ceased when key personnel left County employment, leaving no active enforcement program in place. 
 
Ms. Harper further clarified that although operational practices had shifted over time, the governing code—
originally adopted in 1990 and amended in 1994—has remained unchanged and continues to assign responsibility 
for solid waste enforcement to the Environmental Health division under Public Health. 
 
Frank Wharregard, Community Development, provided additional context regarding prior organizational changes. 
He explained that a former Board action had moved a solid waste-related position from Public Health to 
Community Development due to overlapping responsibilities with code enforcement and land use. However, that 
action was not codified through ordinance, and with the departure of the individual who held the role, the function 
effectively reverted back to Public Health as outlined in County code. He emphasized that Community 
Development does not possess the technical expertise required to supervise solid waste enforcement activities, 
which often involve environmental health considerations such as contamination, sewage, and public health 
hazards. 
 
Janet Fredrickson, Public Health, described more recent administrative changes, including the reassignment of 
solid waste responsibilities and the elimination of associated staff positions. She noted that Public Health currently 
lacks both the staffing and funding necessary to resume enforcement activities and that the prior structure relied

on a combination of funding sources, including grants and discretionary revenue streams that are no longer fully 
available. 
 
Ruth Nelson, Finance Director, provided clarification on available funding sources. She explained that 
discretionary solid waste revenues collected from franchise agreements are currently deposited into the General 
Fund and used to support overall County operations. Additional funding sources, such as state solid waste grants, 
require annual application and are not guaranteed. She noted that the County had previously received substantial 
grant funding for enforcement activities, but those funds were reduced when the program ceased operations. 
 
Trent Carpenter provided further insight into grant funding opportunities, indicating that while the County may 
still be eligible to apply for solid waste grants, prior funding cycles were impacted due to the absence of an active 
program. He suggested that re-establishing a program would improve the County’s ability to secure future funding. 
 
The Board engaged in extensive discussion regarding where solid waste enforcement should be housed 
organizationally. Commissioners acknowledged that there is no single “perfect” placement, as both Public Health 
and Community Development present advantages and challenges. However, it was broadly recognized that Public 
Health aligns most closely with the intent of the existing code and the technical nature of the work, particularly 
with respect to environmental hazards such as sewage, contamination, and vector control. 
 
Discussion also addressed operational challenges within the current code framework, including the requirement 
that complaints be submitted by three unrelated individuals within a defined proximity before enforcement action 
can be initiated. Commissioners noted that this requirement may limit the County’s ability to respond to legitimate 
concerns reported by individual residents and may warrant future review and potential amendment. 
 
Human Resources, through Michelle Shaw, indicated that the County could begin the process of re-establishing 
the program by developing or updating a job description and initiating recruitment. Consideration was also given 
to potential union requirements and hiring procedures that may apply. 
 
Throughout the discussion, Commissioners emphasized that the most immediate priority is not necessarily 
determining the permanent organizational placement of the program, but rather restoring enforcement capability 
to address ongoing public health and safety concerns. It was acknowledged that structural adjustments could be 
made in the future as the program is re-established and evaluated. 
 
Following discussion, the Board reached consensus on the following next steps: Direct staff to pursue available 
grant funding opportunities for solid waste enforcement, Develop or update a job description for a solid waste 
enforcement position, Bring forward a Personnel Requisition (PR) for Board consideration and Prepare a letter of 
intent from the Board of Commissioners to support program re-establishment and funding efforts
