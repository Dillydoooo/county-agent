2. Department Business 
a. Sheriff Office 
1. Comprehensive Radio Communications Review 
Dave Daniel, Sheriff, presented concerns regarding the County’s radio communication system, noting ongoing issues with 
reliability, including intermittent outages, static, and degraded communication between deputies and dispatch. He explained 
that these issues have impacted operations, particularly during winter conditions, and pose a significant public safety 
concern. Sheriff Daniel introduced Matt Weinberger, Senior Sales Representative with Day Wireless Systems, who provided 
an overview of the County’s current communication infrastructure. Mr. Weinberger explained that much of the existing 
equipment is between 15 to 20 years old and is no longer supported by manufacturers. He noted that outdated infrastructure, 
including mountaintop repeater systems and backhaul equipment, contributes to inconsistent coverage and system failures. 
Mr. Weinberger outlined a proposed initial phase consisting of a comprehensive system assessment, including inspection 
of mountaintop sites, evaluation of equipment condition, and collection of performance data. He explained that this 
assessment would identify system deficiencies, coverage gaps, and potential short-term improvements, as well as provide a 
foundation for long-term planning. The assessment would also include documentation review and development of 
preliminary cost estimates and timelines for future upgrades. The Board engaged in discussion regarding system coverage 
gaps, long-term infrastructure planning, and the potential for integrating newer technologies. Concerns were raised about 
reliance on unsupported equipment and the risks associated with communication failures during emergency situations. 
Commissioners emphasized the importance of obtaining accurate data to inform future decisions and noted that the 
assessment would provide a necessary first step in addressing system deficiencies. Discussion also included coordination 
with existing engineering efforts and the importance of maintaining County control over communication infrastructure while 
evaluating potential upgrades. Commissioner Martin made a motion to approve the $29,5000.00 cost for Day Wireless 
Survey. Commissioner Richardson seconded the motion. Upon roll call, Commissioner Richardson – yes, Commissioner 
Martin – yes, Commissioner Smith – yes. Motion carried 3-0 
2. Amendment No. 1 to Oregon State Marine Board Intergovernmental Cooperative Agreement in the 
Amount of $149,527.00 
Dave Daniel, Sheriff, presented Amendment No. 1 to the Oregon State Marine Board Intergovernmental Cooperative 
Agreement. He explained that earlier in the year, funding for the County’s marine patrol program had been reduced due to 
contractual issues between the Oregon State Marine Board and the U.S. Coast Guard related to federal immigration 
language. Sheriff Daniel reported that those issues have since been resolved, allowing the Marine Board to restore previously 
reduced funding. The amendment reflects reinstatement of approximately 33% of funding that had initially been lost, 
increasing the total funding available for the marine deputy position. The Board discussed the importance of maintaining 
funding for marine patrol services and acknowledged the resolution of the prior funding issue. Commissioner Martin made 
a motion to approve the Amendment No. 1 to Oregon State Marine Board Intergovernmental Cooperative Agreement in the 
Amount of $105,000.00-149,527.00. Commissioner Richardson seconded the motion. Upon roll call, Commissioner 
Richardson – yes, Commissioner Martin – yes, Commissioner Smith – yes. Motion carried 3-0 
3. Oregon Department of Emergency Management Performance Grant Program Agreement in the 
Amount of $80,661.00. 
Dave Daniel, Sheriff, presented a grant agreement with the Oregon Department of Emergency Management in the amount 
of $80,661. He explained that the funding supports emergency management operations and coordination efforts within the 
County. The Board discussed the importance of maintaining effective communication and coordination within emergency 
management functions, particularly in light of recent operational challenges. Commissioner Richardson made a motion to 
approve the Oregon Department of Emergency Management Performance Grant Program Agreement in the Amount of 
$80,661.00. Commissioner Martin seconded the motion. Upon roll call, Commissioner Richardson – yes, Commissioner 
Martin – yes, Commissioner Smith – yes. Motion carried 3-0.

b. Community Justice 
1. Contract for Personal Services with OnTrack, Inc. and Josephine County Community Justice for 
Behavioral Health Deflection Grant Services in the Amount of $62,000.00  
Scott Hyde, Community Justice Director, presented a contract with OnTrack, Inc. to provide behavioral health 
deflection services. He explained that the program is designed to support individuals transitioning through the 
justice system by providing access to low-barrier housing and supportive services. Mr. Hyde noted that the 
contract term runs from July 1, 2025, through August 31, 2027, and is funded through a grant in the amount of 
$62,000 Commissioner Martin made a motion to approve the Contract for Personal Services with OnTrack, Inc. and 
Josephine County Community Justice for Behavioral Health Deflection Grant Services in the Amount of $62,000.00. 
Commissioner Richardson seconded the motion. Upon roll call, Commissioner Richardson – yes, Commissioner Martin – 
yes, Commissioner Smith – yes. Motion carried 3-0. 
 
 
      2. Contract for Personal Services with Welcome Home Oregon and Josephine County Community 
Justice for Behavioral Health Deflection Grant Services in the Amount of $308,700.00  
Mr. Hyde presented a second contract with Welcome Home Oregon to provide additional transitional housing services, 
including approximately 14 beds for individuals in need of housing support. The Board discussed the role of these services 
in addressing behavioral health needs and supporting community stability. Commissioner Martin made a motion to approve 
the Contract for Personal Services with Welcome Home Oregon and Josephine County Community Justice for Behavioral 
Health Deflection Grant Services in the Amount of $308,700.00. Commissioner Richardson seconded the motion. Upon 
roll call, Commissioner Richardson – yes, Commissioner Martin – yes, Commissioner Smith – yes. Motion carried 3-0
