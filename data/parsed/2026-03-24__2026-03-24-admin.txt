Liability Company

funds and a request for payment from Evergreen Federal Bank. Mr. Hicks explained that the situation involved

Lottery funding, thereby raising compliance and audit concerns. The Board discussed the importance of ensuring

7. Annual Records Disposition Process 
Commissioner Richardson raised concerns regarding records retention practices across County departments, noting 
that some records were being retained beyond statutory requirements and potentially creating unnecessary

c. Forestry 
1. Extension of Suspension of Order 2025-012; In the Matter of Acceptance of Applications for Mineral 
Exploration and Mining Leases 
David Streeter, Forestry Director, presented a request to extend the current suspension of Order 2025-012, 
which relates to the acceptance of applications for mineral exploration and mining leases on County-owned 
lands. Mr. Streeter explained that the existing ordinance has presented legal vulnerabilities and has contributed 
to ongoing litigation, prompting the need for further review and potential revision. He noted that previous 
efforts to revise the ordinance involved both legal counsel and the Mining Advisory Committee; however, those 
efforts have not resulted in consensus, with differing perspectives on how the ordinance should balance County 
protections and industry interests. Mr. Streeter emphasized that continuing to accept applications under the 
current framework could expose the County to additional legal risk. The Board engaged in discussion regarding 
whether to revise the ordinance or eliminate it altogether in favor of a case-by-case review process. 
Commissioners expressed concern about repeatedly extending the suspension without making a definitive 
policy decision, while also acknowledging the need to avoid premature action while legal and policy 
considerations remain unresolved. Ultimately, the Board agreed that a short-term extension would allow time 
for further coordination between Forestry, Legal Counsel, and potentially outside consultants, while 
maintaining protection against new applications during that period. Commissioner Richardson made a motion to 
Extend the suspension of Order 2025-012; In the Matter of Acceptance for Mineral Exploration and Mining Leases retro 
active to todays date and three months forward.  The motion was seconded by Commissioner Martin. Upon roll call; 
Commissioner Richardson – yes, Commissioner Martin – yes, Commissioner Smith – yes. Motion carried 3-0.

d. Public Works 
1. WECO invoice in the Amount of $44,3377.72 
Richard Pike, Public Works Manager, presented an invoice for fuel purchases totaling $44,377.72. Mr. Pike 
explained that the invoice primarily reflects diesel and unleaded fuel used in ongoing County operations and 
noted that such expenses are recurring and subject to market fluctuations. The Board acknowledged that fuel 
costs represent a significant and ongoing operational expense and discussed the broader financial implications 
of fuel price volatility. Mr. Pike provided additional information regarding potential strategies to manage this 
volatility, including the option of entering into fuel price hedging agreements for diesel. He explained that while 
hedging could provide budget certainty, it also carries risk, particularly if market prices decline after a fixed rate 
is established. Commissioners engaged in discussion regarding the purpose of hedging, emphasizing that its 
value lies in creating predictable budgeting conditions rather than attempting to forecast market trends. It was 
noted that other industries, such as airlines, commonly hedge a portion of their fuel costs to mitigate financial 
risk. The Board expressed interest in further evaluating this option but requested additional data, including 
historical pricing trends and projected budget impacts. Board directed staff to gather additional information and 
return for further discussion on fuel hedging strategies.
