2. Lease Renewal at 530 E. River Street (IV Pool) with Art and Science Kids, LLC 
Tamra Martin, Fairgrounds Director, and Helene Lulich, Property Manager, presented a request to renew the lease 
for the Ivy Pool property located at 530 E. River Street. Staff explained that the property is currently in a 
dilapidated condition and is subject to existing grant encumbrances that limit how the property can be used or 
disposed of. The lease area excludes the building and former pool structure, both of which remain secured and 
inaccessible due to safety concerns. Staff described the existing relationship with Art and Science Kids, LLC, 
noting that the organization has been utilizing the parking lot and surrounding grounds for educational purposes, 
including greenhouse activities. Their presence has resulted in regular monitoring of the site and has helped deter 
vandalism and transient activity. Additionally, staff noted that the organization has informally maintained portions 
of the property through its use. Discussion included questions from the Board regarding the scope of the lease, 
property boundaries, maintenance responsibilities, and safety concerns related to the building. Staff confirmed 
that the building and pool area are fenced, secured, and not accessible to the lessee. It was also noted that the lease 
includes termination provisions should the County need to take action on the property in the future. Staff further 
explained that a separate, longer-term issue remains regarding the property’s encumbrance tied to a Land and 
Water Conservation Fund grant, which would require a formal conversion process before the property could be 
repurposed or sold. That matter is not part of the current lease action and will require future Board discussion. 
The request before the Board was limited to a two-year lease extension 
 
Commissioner Martin made a motion to approve the Lease Renewal at 530 E. River Street (IV Pool) with Art and 
Science Kids, LLC seconded by Commissioner Richardson, Upon Roll Call, Commissioner Richardson – yes, 
Commissioner Martin – yes, Commissioner Smith – yes. Motion carried 3-0. 
 
3. Consideration of Approval of Accredited Representatives of the News Media 
Wally Hicks, Legal Counsel, presented discussion regarding the policy governing accredited representatives of 
the news media, particularly as it relates to participation in executive sessions. He explained that the policy 
requires acknowledgment of legal obligations, including limitations on disclosure, recognition of attorney-client 
privilege, and understanding of subpoena and testimony requirements. The Board discussed the implementation 
of the policy and whether requiring written acknowledgment provides additional value compared to verbal notice 
at the beginning of executive sessions. Legal Counsel emphasized that written acknowledgment offers additional 
protection to the County by clearly documenting understanding of legal obligations. After discussion, 
Commissioners expressed a desire to review the policy further before acting. The Board has tabled this matter.  
 
Following discussion, Legal Counsel recommended deferring executive session until the policy regarding 
accredited media representatives is finalized to ensure consistent application of procedures. 
 
Executive Session was not held. Matters under executive session 4a and 4b are tabled.

5. Approval to move Sale Documents for County Real Property located at 210 Tacoma to the April 8th, 
2026 Weekly Business Session 
Helene Lulich, Property Manager, presented a request to move forward with sale documents for County-owned 
property located at 210 Tacoma Street to the April 8, 2026 Weekly Business Session. She explained that the 
buyer has requested an expedited closing timeline and that staff is working to finalize all necessary 
documentation. Ms. Lulich indicated that if all documents are completed in time, the matter will be scheduled 
for Board consideration on April 8; otherwise, it will be brought forward at the next available meeting. 
Discussion included designation of a signatory for transaction documents. Legal Counsel advised that, while the 
Board Order and deed should be signed by the full Board, it would be more efficient to designate a single 
signatory for the remaining closing documents. The Board agreed to designate the Chair as the authorized 
signatory for closing documents. 
 
Commissioner Martin made a motion to approve the to move Sale Documents for County Real Property located 
at 210 Tacoma to the April 8th, 2026 Weekly Business Session to designate Chair Smith as the signatory for 
closing documents, seconded by Commissioner Richardson, Upon Roll Call, Commissioner Richardson – yes, 
Commissioner Martin – yes, Commissioner Smith – yes. Motion carried 3-0.
